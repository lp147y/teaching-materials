Week 1 · Understanding the causes

Causes of incivility

Incivility rarely arises in isolation. It is often a symptom of organisational and environmental pressures — and of cultures where it goes unchecked. Tap each cause below to reveal the underlying drivers.

Click any card to flip

A stressful environment

How do organisational pressures spill onto colleagues?

Reveal
Cause 01

Poor or stressful environment

Workplace incivility can be the result of an already poor or stressful working environment, where organisational pressures lead employees to direct incivilities towards colleagues. Those being treated badly themselves, working under pressure, or putting in long hours may take out their stress on others.

Organisational silence

Why do incivilities go unchallenged and become "the norm"?

Reveal
Cause 02

Silence and normalisation

Incivilities may be seen as the norm and go largely unchallenged, leading to environments where people are afraid to speak up — known as organisational silence. Concerns get dismissed as "just how things are", and the absence of challenge allows the behaviour to embed itself in the culture.

Mismatched team members

What happens when colleagues simply don't fit together?

Reveal
Cause 03

Mismatch between people

Some incivility stems from a mismatch of team members. While you cannot force colleagues to like each other, ensuring everyone is respectful towards one another is imperative. Personal compatibility is optional — professional respect is not.

Knowing — but choosing otherwise

What about people who simply choose to behave badly?

Reveal
Cause 04

Deliberate behaviour

One of the most common causes is employees who know what constitutes acceptable and unacceptable behaviour but still choose to act incivilly. This is the most difficult type to deal with — policing and changing entrenched behaviour can be almost impossible without strong leadership and clear consequences.

Unintentional incivility

What about people who don't realise they are being rude?

Reveal
Cause 05

Lack of awareness

Some people don't realise their behaviour is rude or offensive — what they say is not intentionally hurtful. This type can often be easier to address: those involved tend to be more open to discussion, feedback and change, and mistakes can be rectified through honest conversation.

The bigger picture

What ties these causes together?

Reveal
Synthesis

Symptoms, not causes

Incivility is rarely about "difficult individuals" alone. It is most often a symptom of organisational stress, weak leadership and silent cultures. Addressing it means addressing the system, not just the behaviour.
